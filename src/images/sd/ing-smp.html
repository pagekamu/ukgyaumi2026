Popular Mexican Tortilla Dishes

Mexican cuisine is famous for using tortillas, which are thin, flat breads made from corn or wheat flour. There are three popular dishes that use tortillas as their main ingredient: Tacos, Quesadillas, and Enchiladas. These dishes are not only delicious but also very easy to customize with various fillings.

First is the Taco. A taco is a small, hand-sized tortilla folded around a filling. People usually fill it with grilled beef, shredded chicken, or beans. To make it fresh, they add toppings like chopped onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice. It is a perfect snack or a light meal that you can eat with your hands.

Second is the Quesadilla. The name comes from "queso," which means cheese in Spanish. To make a quesadilla, you put a lot of cheese inside a folded tortilla and grill it until the cheese melts. Sometimes people add mushrooms or pieces of chicken. It is warm, crunchy outside, and very gooey on the inside.

Finally, there are Enchiladas. Unlike tacos, enchiladas are rolled tortillas filled with chicken or beans, then placed in a baking dish. They are covered with a savory chili sauce and sprinkled with cheese, then baked in the oven. Because they are served with a lot of sauce, people usually eat enchiladas using a fork and a knife.

My Home Sweet Home

I live in a beautiful house in Central Java. It is a traditional Javanese house called a Joglo. My house is mostly made of strong teak wood. It looks very unique and artistic compared to the modern houses in my neighborhood.

The most interesting part of my house is the roof. The roof is very tall and shaped like a mountain. In the middle of the house, there are four main wooden pillars called Soko Guru. These pillars are very strong because they support the heavy roof. My father says that these pillars represent strength and balance.

In front of the house, there is a wide-open area called the Pendopo. We don’t have walls there, so the air feels very fresh and cool. My family usually uses the Pendopo to welcome guests or to practice traditional dances. Behind the Pendopo, there is the Pringgitan and the inner rooms for sleeping.

I love my Joglo house because it is cool even when the weather is hot outside. It is not just a place to live; it is also part of our great culture. I always help my parents to clean the wooden carvings to keep them beautiful.

One sunny morning, a Buffalo was walking near the river. He heard a loud cry for help. He followed the sound and found a Crocodile trapped under a heavy fallen tree branch. The Crocodile looked weak and pleaded, "Please, kind Buffalo, help me! This branch is crushing my back. I promise I will not hurt you."

The Buffalo felt sorry for the Crocodile. He used his strong horns to lift the heavy branch and freed the Crocodile. However, as soon as the Crocodile was free, he suddenly bit the Buffalo’s leg. Suddenly, a Mousedeer arrived. He listened to their stories. "Wait," the Mousedeer said calmly. "I cannot believe that such a heavy branch could be lifted by a Buffalo. The Buffalo then placed the heavy branch back onto the Crocodile’s back.

"Now, Buffalo, let's go!" the Mousedeer whispered. The Crocodile realized he was trapped again and started crying for help, but the Mousedeer and the Buffalo walked away. The Mousedeer said, "Remember, Buffalo, we should help others, but we must also be careful with those who are dishonest."
